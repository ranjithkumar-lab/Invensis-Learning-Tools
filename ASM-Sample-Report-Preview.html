Concepts you studied

KNEW IT

Sprint

A fixed timebox, typically 1 to 4 weeks, during which the team delivers a Done, potentially releasable Increment. Sprints are the same length back-to-back with no gaps between them.

KNEW IT

Scrum Master

A servant-leader for the Scrum Team who facilitates the events, coaches self-management, removes impediments, and protects the team from outside disruption. Accountable for the effectiveness of Scrum itself.

KNEW IT

User story

A short, plain-language description of a feature from the user's perspective. "As a <user>, I want <goal> so that <benefit>." Used to capture a backlog item that fits inside a Sprint.

KNEW IT

Product Owner

Accountable for maximising the value of the product. Owns and orders the Product Backlog and is the single voice on what the team builds next.

KNEW IT

Sprint Planning

The event that kicks off a Sprint. The team sets a Sprint Goal, selects Product Backlog items it forecasts it can complete, and shapes an initial plan for delivering them.

KNEW IT

Daily Scrum

A 15-minute daily event where Developers inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt their plan for the next 24 hours. Held at the same time and place.

KNEW IT

Sprint Retrospective

An event closing the Sprint where the team inspects how it worked (people, relationships, process, tools) and plans specific improvements for the next Sprint.

KNEW IT

Definition of Done (DoD)

A shared checklist of what "complete" means for any Increment. Consistent across the team, evolves over time.

KNEW IT

Empiricism

The idea underpinning Scrum that knowledge comes from experience and decisions are based on what is observed. Implemented through the three pillars of Transparency, Inspection, and Adaptation.

ALMOST

Velocity

The average amount of work, typically in story points, a team completes per Sprint. Used as a forecasting input rather than a productivity target.

ALMOST

Burndown chart

A visualisation of remaining work in a Sprint or release over time. Used to inspect trend and prompt conversations, not to enforce a schedule.

ALMOST

Story points

A relative unit of estimation reflecting effort, complexity, and uncertainty of a backlog item. Deliberately not tied to hours or days.

ALMOST

Backlog Refinement

Ongoing activity where the Scrum Team clarifies, splits, and estimates upcoming Product Backlog items so they are Ready for a future Sprint.

DIDN'T KNOW

WSJF

Weighted Shortest Job First. Prioritises by dividing (business value + urgency + risk reduction) by job size. Common in SAFe.

DIDN'T KNOW

Little's Law

Average cycle time = WIP / Throughput. Mathematical foundation for why WIP limits improve delivery speed.
